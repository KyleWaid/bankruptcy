## SUMMARY OF CORE FINDINGS

Hillary Ann Waid (DOB 7/23/1982, DOD 3/16/2026) was receiving care across at least three separate healthcare systems in her final year. Coordination between these systems does not appear to have occurred. Her husband Kyle Waid — primary caregiver, sole income, parent to their three minor children — made two separate contacts with the healthcare system during her final weeks warning of ongoing suicidality. ## PRELIMINARY MALPRACTICE ANALYSIS — FRAMEWORK

### Potential defendants:
1. **M Health Fairview Ridges** — for the February 2026 discharge decision and inadequate safety planning
2. **Attending physicians** from the February admission
3. **Mental Health Concierge / Intensive Outpatient Program** — for failure to escalate post-discharge outreach
4. **The Remedy / Kellie LeBlanc, DNP** — for polypharmacy / gabapentin prescribing in a patient with active severe alcohol dependence without apparent care coordination
5. **Mobile crisis responder** — for refusal to respond to Kyle's post-discharge call reporting decompensation

### Elements of claim:

**Duty:** Established — patient-provider relationship, hospital duty of care, ongoing treatment relationship with The Remedy, legal obligation of mobile crisis response under DHS guidelines.

**Breach:**
- Discharge within 48 hours of serious suicide attempt
- Safety plan excluding primary caregiver spouse
- Failure to implement means restriction for multi-medication pharmacy in home
- Continued gabapentin prescription in a patient with documented alcohol dependence and fresh post-overdose status
- Refusal to respond to documented post-discharge decompensation when reported by family
- Failure to escalate to in-person welfare check or involuntary hold reconsideration when patient did not respond to three follow-up calls and letters

**Causation:** Hillary completed suicide by ligature hanging 26 days after M Health Fairview discharge. Within that window, Kyle placed a crisis call that was declined. The hospital's own post-discharge outreach (3 calls, letters) was not escalated when she did not respond. Each of these decision points represents a causal link in the chain.

**Damages (Minn. Stat. §573.02):**
- Loss of spouse (Kyle)
- Loss of mother for 3 minor children (substantial component)
- Funeral/burial expenses
- Medical expenses
- Pecuniary loss (homemaker services — legally compensable even without earned income)
- Loss of companionship, comfort, guidance (children's component is primary)

Minnesota does not cap non-economic damages in wrongful death.

### Preliminary valuation
Post-discharge suicide cases in Minnesota with comparable fact patterns — inadequate discharge planning, documented family warnings, subsequent completion — commonly range from low-mid six figures to low seven figures depending on jurisdiction, defendant's insurance coverage, and specific documentary record.

The estate's bankruptcy status (Case 24-32819) creates additional structural considerations:
- A portion of recovery allocated to minor children under §573.02 may be protected from creditors
- The claim itself is an asset of the bankruptcy estate requiring amendment of Schedule B
- Recovery would likely impact plan modification calculations

## LEGAL ISSUES

### 401(k) Protection IN the Account
- ERISA (29 USC 1056(d)): anti-alienation provision
- Federal bankruptcy law (11 USC 522): retirement fund exemption
- Minnesota law (Minn. Stat. 550.37 subd. 24): fully exempt, unlimited
- Patterson v. Shumate, 504 U.S. 753 (1992): ERISA plans excluded from estate
- **The money is 100% protected as long as it stays in the 401(k)**

### Once Withdrawn — Protection May Be Lost
- Withdrawn funds are cash in a bank account — no longer ERISA-protected
- In re Mati (Bankr. D. Minn.) — withdrawn retirement funds may lose exempt status
- Trustee can argue withdrawal proceeds should go to plan
- Cash is property of the estate under 11 USC 1306(a)

### Court Approval Required
- New 401(k) loans during Ch.13 generally require court approval
- Considered new debt under 11 USC 1305
- Hardship withdrawals also problematic without court knowledge
- Unauthorized borrowing viewed as compliance violation
- Can trigger trustee objections and potential dismissal

### Trustee's Potential Arguments
1. Withdrawn funds lost exempt status → should go to plan
2. Unauthorized new debt (if loan) → compliance violation
3. Reduced disposable income (if loan repayments) → less for creditors
4. Bad faith → pattern of depleting protected assets

## 4. Debtor Rights When Circumstances Change

### Option A: Plan Modification (11 USC §1329)
- Primary remedy
- Can reduce monthly payment, extend duration (max 5 years from first payment), reduce unsecured %
- Death of spouse significantly changes means test and disposable income
- Must still meet §1325(a) requirements

### Option B: Hardship Discharge (11 USC §1328(b))
- When modification is not practicable
- Requirements: (1) failure not debtor's fault, (2) unsecured creditors got at least Ch. 7 amount, (3) modification not practicable
- Death of spouse = classic qualifying circumstance
- More limited than full Ch. 13 discharge — doesn't cover §523(a) debts

### Option C: Voluntary Dismissal (11 USC §1307(b))
- Near-absolute right (unless converted from Ch. 7)
- Returns to pre-bankruptcy status quo
- Liens restored, stay ends, creditors resume collection

### Option D: Convert to Chapter 7 (11 USC §1307(a))
- Absolute right if not previously converted from Ch. 7
- Appropriate if no longer have regular income for a plan

### Option E: Temporary Payment Suspension
- Not statutory — practical accommodation
- Some trustees/courts allow reduced/suspended payments while modification pending
- Attorney should contact trustee's office immediately

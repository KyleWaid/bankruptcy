## 1. Plan Duration & Calculations

- **Below median income**: Plan can be 36-60 months
- **Above median income**: Plan must be 60 months (the "applicable commitment period")
- Below median: payments based on **actual disposable income** (Schedule I and J)
- Above median: payments calculated via **means test** (Form 122C) using IRS standards
- Plan must satisfy **best interest of creditors test** (11 USC §1325(a)(4))
- Priority debts (taxes, domestic support) must be paid in full

**Current Monthly Income (CMI):**
- Average monthly gross income over 6 months preceding filing
- Annualized and compared to Census median for state + household size
- **Social Security income is EXCLUDED from CMI by statute** (11 USC §101(10A)(B))

## 2. Minnesota Exemptions (MN Stat Chapter 550)

Minnesota is an **opt-out state** — must use state exemptions, not federal (Minn. Stat. §550.36).

| Category | Statute | Amount |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Homestead | §510.01, 510.02 | ~$450,000 |
| Vehicle | §550.37 subd. 12a | $5,000 equity |
| Household goods/furniture | §550.37 subd. 4(a) | $11,250 |
| Tools of trade | §550.37 subd. 5 | $13,000 |
| Wedding rings | §550.37 subd. 4(g) | Unlimited (1 per spouse) |
| Retirement (401k, 403b, pensions) | §550.37 subd. 24 | **Fully exempt / unlimited** |
| IRAs | §550.37 subd. 24 | ~$1,512,350 (federal cap) |
| Life insurance proceeds (to spouse/child) | §550.37 subd. 23 | ~$46,000 per beneficiary |
| Unmatured life insurance cash value | §550.37 subd. 10 | ~$9,200 |
| Group life insurance | — | Generally fully exempt |
| Public benefits (welfare, workers comp, unemployment, VA) | — | Fully exempt |

**No wildcard exemption in Minnesota.**

*Note: Dollar amounts are periodically adjusted — verify current figures.*

## 3. 401(k) Loans & Hardship Withdrawals During Chapter 13

### 401(k) Loans
- Generally **NOT permitted without court approval**
- Creates new debt obligation reducing disposable income
- Trustee can and often will object
- Some courts allow if debtor shows necessity and creditors aren't harmed
- Treated as post-petition credit transaction requiring court approval (11 USC §364)

### Hardship Withdrawals
- Converts exempt property (401k) into potentially **non-exempt cash**
- Trustee can argue withdrawal proceeds should be contributed to plan
- Courts are divided on treatment

**⚠️ CRITICAL: Always seek court approval BEFORE any 401(k) loan or hardship withdrawal during Chapter 13.**

## 4. Death of Spouse During Active Chapter 13

### Non-filing spouse dies (THIS CASE):
- Filing debtor continues the case
- Should file **modified plan** reflecting loss of household income and any new income/assets
- Motion to modify under 11 USC §1329

### Options available:
1. **Modify the plan** (§1329) — reduce payments based on changed circumstances
2. **Convert to Chapter 7** (§1307(a)) — debtor has absolute right to convert
3. **Dismiss the case** (§1307(b))
4. **Hardship discharge** (§1328(b)) — if:
   - Failure to complete plan is due to circumstances beyond debtor's control
   - Unsecured creditors received at least as much as they would in Ch. 7
   - Modification is not practicable
   - **Death of spouse is a classic qualifying circumstance**

## 5. Social Security Survivor Benefits

- **42 USC §407(a)**: SS benefits are NOT subject to execution, levy, attachment, garnishment, or other legal process — **absolute federal protection**
- **Excluded from CMI** for means test (11 USC §101(10A)(B))
- **Trustee CANNOT touch Social Security survivor benefits**
- Eighth Circuit (covers MN): SS income cannot be involuntarily committed to the plan
- A debtor who voluntarily proposes to use SS income may be held to that commitment

**⚠️ IMPORTANT: Keep SS survivor benefits in a SEPARATE dedicated bank account to avoid commingling/tracing disputes.**

## 6. Life Insurance Proceeds During Chapter 13

- Exempt portion protected under Minn. Stat. §550.37 subd. 23 (~$46,000/beneficiary)
- Non-exempt portion becomes property of estate under 11 USC §1306(a)
- Must report to trustee
- May need to modify plan to increase payments if proceeds exceed exemption
- Group life insurance generally fully exempt

## 7. Plan Modification (11 USC §1329)

**Who can request:** Debtor, trustee, or unsecured creditor
**When:** Any time after confirmation but before completion of payments
**Term limit:** Cannot exceed 5 years from first payment date (§1329(c))

**Recognized grounds:**
- Change in income (loss, increase)
- Change in expenses (medical, new necessities)
- **Death of spouse** — textbook basis for modification
- Receipt of inheritance, insurance proceeds
- Change in secured debt

**Process:** File motion to modify with updated Schedules I and J, propose modified plan.

- Approximately **$120,000-$126,000** annually (as of late 2024/early 2025)
- Based on household size and state, not marital status
- Single parent + 3 children = household of 4

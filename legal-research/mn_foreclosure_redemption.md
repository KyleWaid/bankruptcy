# Minnesota Foreclosure & Redemption Rights After Relief From Stay
## What Happens When Mortgage Servicer Lifts the Stay

**DISCLAIMER: This is legal research/information, not legal advice.**

---

## KEY POINT: Relief From Stay ≠ Losing the House

When a mortgage servicer obtains relief from the automatic stay in Chapter 13, it ONLY means:
- The bankruptcy stay is lifted for that creditor
- They can BEGIN the foreclosure process
- Minnesota's full foreclosure protections STILL APPLY

---

## MINNESOTA FORECLOSURE TIMELINE (After Relief From Stay)

### Step 1: Pre-Sale Reinstatement Right
- You can cure the default at ANY TIME before the sheriff's sale
- Pay: all missed payments + costs + fees
- This STOPS the foreclosure entirely
- Minn. Stat. §580.30

### Step 2: Notice Requirements
- Servicer must serve notice on occupants at least **4 weeks** before sale
- Legal notice must be published at least **6 weeks** before sale
- "Dual tracking" prohibited — cannot foreclose while reviewing loan modification application
- Must pause sale if modification application submitted 7+ business days before scheduled sale
- Must allow 60 days after modification denial before resuming foreclosure

### Step 3: Sheriff's Sale
- Public auction
- Lender typically bids the amount owed (credit bid)

### Step 4: REDEMPTION PERIOD — YOUR MAJOR PROTECTION

**Standard: 6 months after sheriff's sale** (Minn. Stat. §580.23)
- You can STAY IN THE HOME during entire redemption period
- You remain in possession
- To redeem: pay the sale price + interest + costs

**Extended: 12 months** if:
- Amount due at time of foreclosure filing < 66⅔% of original principal (i.e., you've paid down more than ⅓)
- Property is over 40 acres
- Property is 10-40 acres in agricultural use

**Shortened: 5 weeks** ONLY if:
- Bank proves in court that property is abandoned/vacant
- You can CONTEST this — if you're living there, it doesn't apply

### Step 5: After Redemption Period Expires
- If you do NOT redeem, title transfers to the buyer (usually the lender)
- You must vacate
- Lender may need to do formal eviction if you don't leave voluntarily

---

## NO DEFICIENCY JUDGMENT IN MOST CASES

- In a nonjudicial foreclosure by advertisement (the standard in MN), the lender CANNOT get a deficiency judgment
- If the home sells for less than what's owed, you don't owe the difference
- Minn. Stat. §582.30

---

## TOTAL TIMELINE: Relief From Stay → Loss of Possession

| Phase | Approximate Time |
|-------|-----------------|
| Relief from stay granted | Day 0 |
| Notice/publication requirements | 4-6 weeks |
| Sheriff's sale | ~6-8 weeks after relief |
| Redemption period | 6 months (or 12 months) |
| **Total minimum** | **~8 months** |
| **If 12-month redemption** | **~14 months** |

---

## STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS

1. **Even after relief from stay, you have TIME** — use it to explore options
2. **Reinstatement before sale** is the cheapest option if you can gather the arrears
3. **During redemption period** you can:
   - Try to sell the house yourself (potentially getting equity)
   - Seek loan modification directly with servicer
   - Arrange alternative housing
   - Save money (you're living payment-free during redemption)
4. **Filing a second bankruptcy** during the redemption period may extend it by 60 days (but serial filing has risks and limitations)

---

## INTERACTION WITH CHAPTER 13

- If your Ch.13 plan was curing mortgage arrears and the servicer got relief from stay, it usually means the plan payments weren't sufficient or weren't being made
- Plan modification under §1329 could potentially address the mortgage arrears before the servicer even files for relief
- Rule 3002.1 (amended Dec 2024) gives you a tool to check mortgage claim status during your plan

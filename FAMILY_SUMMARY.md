# Kyle's Situation — Summary for Family

---

In October 2024, I filed for Chapter 13 bankruptcy to protect our home and get back on track financially. Chapter 13 is a repayment plan — I keep everything and pay what I can over 3-5 years. I earn $150,000 a year as a software engineer. I have the income. What I haven't had is the ability to keep up with a plan that was designed for a life that no longer exists.

## What Happened

She struggled with alcoholism and spent over $700 a month on alcohol from our bank account. I did everything — worked full time, raised three kids, managed every bill, and dealt with her addiction alone. I never asked for help because I didn't know how.

When I filed bankruptcy, the plan assumed two adults in the household. There was always only one.

On March 16, 2026, Hillary took her own life. I am now a single father of three children — Isla (15), Owen (13), and Eliza (11).

They bounced my payments twice — during the week Hillary died — even though their own email proved they had my correct account number. They are now trying to raise my monthly payment by over $300 to cover an escrow deficit that their own accounting created. I have documented all of this.

The IRS immediately accepted the one year where I owed them money. They rejected the two years where they owe me money. They gave me a phone number to resolve it that never works. They now require Hillary to appear in person for identity verification — which is impossible. Meanwhile, they are collecting from me through the bankruptcy plan every month.

I asked my attorney twice if this was OK. They said yes, in writing. Legal research shows they were wrong — court approval was required. Every dollar I withdrew cost me roughly 40 cents in taxes and penalties. I have destroyed my retirement savings on bad legal advice.

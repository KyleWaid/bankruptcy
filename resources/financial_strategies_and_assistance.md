## 2. DAVE RAMSEY — RELEVANT POSITIONS

### On Chapter 13:
- Not a fan — calls it "a long and painful experience"
- But for someone already IN Chapter 13, his financial principles still apply

### On the "Big Shovel":
- Maximize the gap between income and expenses
- During Ch.13: maximize income, minimize expenses, stay current on plan payments

### On 401(k) Borrowing — ADAMANTLY AGAINST:
- You lose compound growth (10-12% average returns)
- If you lose your job, loan becomes due immediately → taxable distribution + 10% penalty
- Considers it one of the worst financial moves possible
- Alternative: build emergency fund, cut expenses, increase income

### On Single Parents:
- Recommends **6-month emergency fund** (vs. 3-month for dual-income households)
- Zero-based budget: every dollar assigned a job before the month begins

### Baby Steps (adapted for during/after Ch.13):
1. $1,000 starter emergency fund (may need to disclose to trustee)
2. During Ch.13, the plan IS your debt payoff — stay current
3. After plan: build 3-6 months expenses as full emergency fund
4. Steps 4-7 come after plan completion

## 3. BUDGETING STRATEGIES DURING CHAPTER 13

### Zero-Based Budgeting
- Assign every dollar of income before the month starts
- Income minus expenses = zero
- Plan payment is a non-negotiable fixed "bill"

### Cash Flow Management
- Pay trustee payment the SAME DAY income arrives
- Keep one simple checking account
- Build even a $50-100 buffer against overdrafts
- Time bill payments to match pay cycles
- Eliminate all non-essential subscriptions

### Side Income — PROCEED WITH CAUTION
- You CAN earn side income during Chapter 13
- You MUST report significant income changes to your attorney
- Trustee reviews income annually via tax returns
- New regular income could trigger plan modification with higher payments
- **Discuss any planned side work with attorney BEFORE starting**
- Failure to disclose = plan dismissal or fraud allegations

### Emergency Expenses Without Raiding Retirement
- Build small disclosed emergency fund ($30-50/month, disclosed to trustee)
- Request plan modification for significant financial changes
- Request temporary payment deferment via court motion
- Court can approve temporary payment reductions
- **Once you withdraw from 401(k), that protection is GONE and cannot be restored**

## 4. SOCIAL SECURITY SURVIVOR BENEFITS — APPLICATION PROCESS

### How to Apply
- **Call SSA: 1-800-772-1213** (TTY: 1-800-325-0778)
- Applications by phone or in person at local SSA office
- **Cannot be completed online as of 2026**
- Expect to schedule appointment 30-60 days out

### Documents Needed
- Deceased parent's death certificate
- Children's birth certificates
- Proof of marriage (if applicable for stepchildren)
- Deceased's Social Security number
- Children's Social Security numbers
- W-2s / self-employment tax returns for deceased (most recent year)
- **Don't delay if missing documents — SSA will help gather what's needed**

### Processing Time
- Typical: **4-8 weeks** after phone interview
- Most report first payment at ~5-6 week mark
- Benefits paid in month FOLLOWING the month due
- **Retroactive payments back to eligibility date** — no money lost during processing
- Lump-sum death benefit of $255 also available

### Monthly Amounts (3 Children)
- Each child eligible for up to **75% of deceased parent's full benefit**
- 3 children raw total = 225% of benefit
- **Family maximum caps at 150-180%** of deceased worker's benefit
- Example: If deceased's benefit was $2,000/month:
  - Raw: 3 × $1,500 = $4,500
  - Capped at ~$3,000-$3,600/month
  - Each child's amount reduced proportionally
- Surviving parent caring for child under 16 may ALSO receive benefits (75% of worker's benefit, subject to same family max)

### Duration
- Benefits continue until each child turns **18**
- Full-time high school student: until age **19**
- Child with disability (began before age 22): can continue indefinitely

### ⚠️ CRITICAL REMINDER: Wife must have had sufficient work credits
- Generally needs 40 credits (~10 years work) OR 6 credits in 3 years before death
- If wife never worked, children may NOT qualify
- **This must be verified before planning around this income**

## 5. MINNESOTA ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS FOR SINGLE FATHERS

### Minnesota Family Investment Program (MFIP)
- Cash assistance + food + childcare + employment services
- Apply: MNbenefits.mn.gov

### Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP)
- Helps pay for childcare while working/in school
- Apply online — no interview required
- Processing: 30-45 days
- Covers children up to age 12 (15 with special needs)
- dcyf.mn.gov/programs-directory/child-care-assistance-program

### Health Insurance
- **MNsure.org** — single application checks Medical Assistance + MinnesotaCare
- Medical Assistance: income under ~138% FPL
- MinnesotaCare: 138-200% FPL
- **Children may qualify at higher income thresholds than adults**

### Energy Assistance (LIHEAP/EAP)
- Pays part of heating bills ($200-$1,400, avg ~$500)
- Crisis benefits to prevent disconnection
- Deadline: May 31, 2026
- **Assets like homes NOT considered**
- mn.gov/commerce/energy/consumer-assistance/energy-assistance-program

### Food Assistance (SNAP)
- MN expanded: gross income limit at **200% FPL**
- Max monthly (2026): ~$1,759 for large households
- Apply: MNbenefits.mn.gov
- Expedited processing (7 days) for emergencies

### Emergency Assistance
- For eviction, utility shutoff, fire/disaster losses
- Apply: MNbenefits.mn.gov (~20 minutes)
- Or in person at county/tribal social services

### One-Call Resources
- **United Way 211**: Call 651-291-0211 or 1-800-543-7709 or text zip to 898-211 (24/7)
- **Help Me Connect**: helpmeconnect.web.health.state.mn.us — state portal for all family services

### ⚠️ INCOME NOTE FOR YOUR SITUATION:
At $145k salary, you likely will NOT qualify for most means-tested programs (SNAP, MFIP, Medical Assistance, Energy Assistance). However:
- Your CHILDREN may qualify for certain programs at different thresholds
- If your net take-home after Ch.13 plan payments, taxes, and 401(k) deductions is drastically lower, some programs may apply
- Child Care Assistance has its own income scale — worth checking
- **Call 211 to get a real assessment based on your actual household situation**

## 6. CREDIT COUNSELING REQUIREMENTS

### Required Course 1: Pre-Filing Credit Counseling
- Must complete within 180 days BEFORE filing ✅ (already done — you filed in 2024)

### Required Course 2: Post-Filing Debtor Education
- Must complete AFTER filing but before discharge
- Chapter 13: certificate due no later than last plan payment or motion for discharge
- Covers: budgeting, money management, use of credit
- Fee: $50 or less; sliding scale/waivers available below 150% FPL
- Must use U.S. Trustee-approved provider: justice.gov/ust

### Beyond Requirements — Ongoing Education
- **EveryDollar app** (Ramsey, free version) — zero-based budgeting tool
- **Financial Peace University** — check local churches for free offerings
- **Credit union financial counseling** — often free for members
- **Lutheran Social Service of MN** — financial counseling beyond basic bankruptcy requirement
- **CFPB free tools**: consumerfinance.gov

### Key Insight:
Ongoing accountability (even informal — trusted friend reviewing budget monthly) significantly improves Chapter 13 completion rates.
